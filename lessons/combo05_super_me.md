# 💪 Combo Lesson 5 — Super Me!

**Built from Question Bank sections:** 3 (About You) + 15 (Body & Health) + 10 (Sports & Exercise)
**Grammar focus:** **should / shouldn't** ("You should drink water. You shouldn't eat too much candy.") + "I'm good at ___ing."
**Spelling set:** *hand, leg, jump, strong, teeth, water*

## 📖 Story: *Super Bin and the Candy Monster*

> In the city, there is a sneaky monster: the **Candy Monster**! He whispers to children: "Eat candy for breakfast! Don't brush your teeth! Watch TV aaaall day! Don't sleep!"
> The children who listen become **weak** and **lazy**. They have **toothaches**. They can't run. They can't play. "Heee heee," laughs the Candy Monster.
> But one boy doesn't listen. His name is **Super Bin**!
> Every morning, Super Bin eats a good breakfast — "Breakfast gives me **energy**!"
> He brushes his teeth twice a day — "My teeth are **stronger** than candy!"
> He runs and jumps and swims — "Exercise makes my **muscles** strong!"
> He sleeps at nine o'clock — "Sleep makes me grow!"
> One day, the Candy Monster comes to Bin's window: "Psst! Candy for breakfast?"
> Super Bin stands up tall: "NO! I **shouldn't** eat candy for breakfast! I **should** eat a real breakfast! And YOU should brush your teeth — your breath is YUCKY!"
> The Candy Monster smells his own breath… "Oh no. It's true." He runs away to find a toothbrush.
> And that is how Super Bin saved the city — with broccoli, a toothbrush, and nine-o'clock bedtime.

**Story questions:**
1. What does the Candy Monster tell children to do? Should they listen?
2. What 4 things does Super Bin do every day?
3. Why is breakfast important? (It gives me…)
4. What did Bin tell the monster? ("You should…!")
5. **About him:** What do YOU do to be strong? What should you do more?

**⭐ The big task — Superhero Rules:** he makes his own poster: "SUPER ___'s 5 RULES" — five should/shouldn't sentences he writes himself ("I should drink water. I shouldn't watch TV all day…"). Hang it on his wall, for real.

## ⏰ 1-Hour Plan

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 0–8 min | Warm-up: quick-fire from sections **3, 15, 10** ("What are you good at?", "Why do we wash our hands?", "How often do you exercise?") |
| 8–18 min | New vocabulary: say → spell → his own sentence |
| 18–30 min | **Story:** read twice — you are the sneaky whispering monster, he is Super Bin (he'll demand to read Bin's "NO!" speech), story questions |
| 30–40 min | **Superhero Rules poster** — 5 should/shouldn't sentences, written and decorated |
| 40–45 min | Spelling round: *hand, leg, jump, strong, teeth, water* + air-writing game |
| 45–58 min | 🎮 Family game: **Superhero Training Camp** (below) |
| 58–60 min | He tests the family: "Daddy, should we eat candy for breakfast?" Family answers in full sentences. Sticker ⭐ |

## 🎮 Family game: Superhero Training Camp (cả nhà cùng chơi)

He is the **Coach**. The family are the trainee superheroes. The coach only speaks English!

1. **Round 1 — Coach commands:** "Jump five times! Touch your toes! Run to the door and back! Stand on one leg!" Family obeys; slowest trainee does one extra jump. (He gives commands = he produces the language.)
2. **Round 2 — Monster Quiz:** One parent plays the Candy Monster saying terrible advice: "You should watch TV all night!" The trainees must shout the correction: "NO! We SHOULDN'T watch TV all night! We SHOULD sleep!" Coach Bin judges who shouted the best sentence.
3. **Round 3 — Good-at show:** each family member shows a "superpower" and says it: "I'm good at cooking!" (Mom), "I'm good at sleeping!" (Dad). He introduces each one like a TV host: "This is Super Mom! She is good at cooking!"
4. **Round 4 — Family Olympics mini:** one-leg standing contest + sock-throw — he commentates with comparatives: "Mommy is stronger than Daddy!" (Full rules in the [Family Game Bank](family_games.md), game 14.)

**Graduation:** everyone gets a superhero name from the Coach and must say their rule: "I am Super Grandma! I should walk every morning!"

## ✅ Success check
1. Makes 5 original should/shouldn't sentences (his poster).
2. Says "I'm good at ___ing" about himself and 2 family members ("She is good at…").
3. Gives 5+ English commands as Coach without help.
4. Spells: *hand, leg, jump, strong, teeth, water*.
