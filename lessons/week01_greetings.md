# Week 1 – All About Me (Giới thiệu bản thân) — Level 2

**Grammar A:** "My name is… / I am 6 years old. / I live in Vietnam. / I have a (dog, sister…). / I like…"
**Grammar B (questions):** "What's your name? How old are you? Where do you live? What do you like?"
**Phonics:** short *a* — cat, hat, map, bag, dad, man

## ⏰ 1-Hour Session Plan

### 0–5 min · 🎵 Warm-up (English only!)
Quick-fire questions — full sentences, two details each (more in the [Question Bank](quickfire_questions.md)):
- "Good morning! How are you today?" → "I'm fine and a little sleepy."
- "What day is it today?" → "It's Tuesday."
- "What day was it yesterday?" → "It was Monday."
- "How's the weather?" → "It's sunny and hot."
- "What are you wearing?" → "I'm wearing a blue T-shirt and shorts."
- "Are you hungry?" → "No, I'm not. I'm thirsty!"
- Follow up one answer with "Why?" — he must give a reason: "…because it's summer."

### 5–12 min · 🗣️ Review + Spelling (no cards needed)
First lesson of Level 2 — test what he knows using the room and your voice:
- **Point test:** point at real things (a red cup, the door, his shirt, three pencils) — he names them in English and spells the easy ones.
- **Translate test:** say a known word in Vietnamese (con mèo, màu xanh, số bảy) — he says it in English, spells it, and makes a sentence: "The cat is black."
- Write words he can't spell on a paper scrap and keep it in a "tricky words" cup — they come back next week.

### 12–25 min · ⭐ New vocabulary
For each of the 12 words: you say it, he repeats, he spells it, then he makes his OWN sentence (help only if stuck). Words he struggles with go in a "tricky pile" to repeat at the end.

### 25–37 min · 📚 Grammar A — Self-introduction
Build his personal introduction, one line at a time, until he can say all 6 lines alone:

> Hello! My name is ___.
> I am six years old.
> I live in ___, Vietnam. I am Vietnamese.
> I have a ___ (mom, dad, brother/sister, pet).
> My favorite ___ is ___.
> My hobby is ___.

Then the challenge: he says it standing up, like a TV presenter, without help. Record it on your phone — he'll love watching it, and you can compare in week 16.

### 37–43 min · 📖 Reading — short *a*
He reads these aloud (write them big on paper):
1. The cat is on the mat.
2. Dad has a black bag.
3. The man has a map and a hat.
4. I am a happy cat!

Point out the short *a* sound in every word: c**a**t, m**a**p, b**a**g.

### 43–48 min · ✏️ Writing
In his English notebook, with the date on top (e.g. *Tuesday, June 10th*):
1. Copy 4 words: **name, live, family, happy**
2. Write 1 sentence from memory: **My name is ___.**

### 48–56 min · 🎮 Game — Grammar B
**Interview the Toy:** He interviews a stuffed animal (you do its voice). He must ask at least 5 questions:
- "What's your name?" — "My name is Bear."
- "How old are you?" — "I am 100 years old!"
- "Where do you live?" — "I live in the forest."
- "What's your favorite food?" — "Honey!"
- "What's your hobby?" — "Sleeping!"

Then he **reports** to you: "His name is Bear. He is 100. He lives in the forest. He likes honey."
(This switch from *my/I* to *his/he* is the real challenge of the week.)

### 56–60 min · 🗨️ Free talk + Wrap-up
He interviews YOU with 3 questions, you answer in full sentences. Sticker ⭐, "See you next time!"

---

## 🏠 During the week (5 minutes a day)
- Day 1–2: he says his 6-line introduction to another family member.
- Day 3–4: he interviews grandma/grandpa/a sibling: name, age, favorite food (translate for them if needed — HE must ask in English).
- Day 5: mini diary — he writes 2 lines: "My name is ___. I am happy today."
- Anytime: spelling challenge in the car — "Spell *cat*! Spell *bag*!"

## ✅ Success check
1. Says the full 6-line self-introduction alone, standing, no help.
2. Asks all 4 questions (name / age / live / like) without prompting.
3. Reports about someone else using *he/she*: "Her name is… She likes…"
4. Reads the 4 short-*a* sentences aloud and writes "My name is ___" from memory.
