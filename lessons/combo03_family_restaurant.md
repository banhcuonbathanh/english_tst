## 📖 Story: *The Best Little Waiter in Hanoi*

> Bin's family has a small restaurant. Tonight, the waiter is sick. "Who can help?" asks Dad.
> "ME!" says Bin. He puts on a small apron. He is the **waiter** now!
> The first **customer** is an old man. "Good evening! Would you like the **menu**?" says Bin, very **polite**.
> "I would like pho, please. How much is it?" — "It's forty thousand dong. Good choice! My grandma makes it. It's **delicious**!"
> Bin carries the hot bowl slowly, slowly… don't drop it… "Here you are!"
> The old man eats everything. "Mmm! This is the best pho in Hanoi! Can I have the **bill**, please?"
> He gives Bin fifty thousand. Bin thinks hard… "Here's your **change** — ten thousand dong!"
> "Correct! And this is for you," the man smiles, and gives Bin a sticker: ⭐ *Best Little Waiter in Hanoi*.

**Story questions:**
1. Why does Bin become the waiter?
2. What does the customer order? How much is it?
3. How much change does Bin give? (50 − 40 = ?)
4. Was Bin polite? What polite words did he say? (Would you like… / Here you are… / please)

## 🎮 Family game: The Family Restaurant (cả nhà cùng chơi)

The kitchen table becomes a restaurant. Use real (safe) food or empty bowls + paper money (or real small notes!).

**Roles — rotate so everyone plays everything:**
- **Waiter** (him first): greets, gives the menu, takes orders, brings "food", makes the bill, gives change.
- **Chef** (Mom/Grandma): waiter must SHOUT the order to the kitchen in English: "One pho and one juice, please!"
- **Customers** (Dad, siblings): must order in full sentences, complain politely ("Excuse me, this soup is too spicy!"), and pay.

**The script he must master:**
> "Good evening! Welcome! Would you like the menu?"
> "What would you like to order?" — "I'd like…, please."
> "Here you are. Enjoy your meal!"
> "Can I have the bill, please?" — "It's ___ thousand dong."
> "Here's your change. Thank you! Come again!"

**Round 2 — Difficult Customers 😈:** Dad orders something NOT on the menu ("I'd like a pizza!" — "Sorry, we don't have pizza. Would you like fried rice?"). Grandma pays with too much money (change practice = math in English). Mom asks "What do you recommend?" — he must answer with *because*: "The pho, because my grandma makes it!"

**Round 3 — He is the customer**, parents are terrible waiters who bring the wrong food — he must complain politely: "Excuse me! I ordered juice, not soup!"
