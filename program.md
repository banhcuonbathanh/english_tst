# 🇬🇧 English Program for a 6-Year-Old — Level 2 (Nâng cao)

**For a child who already knows:** greetings, colors, numbers 1–10, basic animals, "It's…", "I like…".
**Goal:** Full sentences, questions & answers, reading short sentences, writing words, real conversations.
**Schedule:** 2–3 sessions per week, **1 hour each**.

---

## ⏰ The 1-Hour Session Structure (Level 2)

| Time | Activity | What to do |
|------|----------|------------|
| 0–5 min | 🎵 **Warm-up** | English only! 8–10 quick-fire questions from the [Question Bank](lessons/quickfire_questions.md). Full sentences, two details each ("It's sunny **and** hot."), plus one *Why?* follow-up. |
| 5–12 min | 🗣️ **Review + Spelling** | No cards needed: say last week's word in Vietnamese OR point at a real object — he says it in English, SPELLS it (c-a-t), and USES it in a sentence. |
| 12–25 min | ⭐ **New vocabulary (10–12 words)** | Teach with real objects, drawings, actions, or photos on your phone. For each word: say it → spell it → make a sentence with it. |
| 25–37 min | 📚 **Grammar A (main pattern)** | Teach the pattern, then HE makes 5 new sentences himself (not just repeating yours). |
| 37–43 min | 📖 **Reading** | He reads 4–6 short sentences aloud from cards or the lesson sheet. Phonics focus on one sound per week. |
| 43–48 min | ✏️ **Writing** | He writes 3–4 of the new words + 1 full sentence in a notebook. |
| 48–56 min | 🎮 **Game with Grammar B (question pattern)** | A game that forces him to ASK questions, not just answer (see Game Bank). |
| 56–60 min | 🗨️ **Free talk + Wrap-up** | 2-minute "interview": he asks YOU 3 questions in English. Sticker, goodbye. |

**Level 2 rules for parents:**
- "English only" during the lesson — if he speaks Vietnamese, gently say "In English, please!"
- Don't accept one-word answers anymore. "Blue" → "It's blue." / "Yes" → "Yes, I do."
- Let him make mistakes and self-correct: repeat his sentence correctly and have him say it again.
- He should ASK as many questions as he answers — asking is the harder skill.

## 📅 16-Week Plan (Theme → Grammar A → Grammar B → Phonics)

| Week | Theme | Grammar A (statements) | Grammar B (questions) | Phonics sound |
|------|-------|------------------------|----------------------|---------------|
| 1 | All About Me | "My name is… I am 6. I live in Vietnam. I have…" | "What's your name? Where do you live?" | short *a* (cat, hat) |
| 2 | My Family & Jobs | "My dad is a doctor. He works in a hospital." | "Who is this? What does he do?" | short *e* (pen, bed) |
| 3 | Numbers 11–50 & Counting | "There are fifteen birds." plurals: -s/-es | "How many ___ are there?" | short *i* (pig, six) |
| 4 | My House & Rooms | Prepositions: "The cat is **on/in/under/next to** the bed." | "Where is the ___?" | short *o* (dog, box) |
| 5 | Daily Routine & Time | "I wake up at 7 o'clock. I brush my teeth." | "What time do you ___?" | short *u* (sun, cup) |
| 6 | Actions Now | Present continuous: "He **is running**. They **are eating**." | "What is he doing?" | *sh* (ship, fish) |
| 7 | Weather & Seasons ☀️ | "It's rainy, **so** we stay inside. I wear a raincoat **because** it's cold." *(use [weather/](weather/) files)* | "What's the weather like in winter? What do you wear?" | *ch* (chair, lunch) |
| 8 | Food & Meals | "I have rice **and** fish for lunch. I **don't** like onions." | "What do you have for breakfast? Do you like ___?" | *th* (this, three) |
| 9 | Animals & Abilities | "A bird **can** fly but it **can't** swim. A monkey is **faster than** a turtle." | "Can a fish climb? Which is bigger?" | long *a* (cake, rain) |
| 10 | Places in Town | "There **is** a park. There **are** two shops. Go straight, turn left." | "Is there a ___? How do I get to ___?" | long *e* (tree, sea) |
| 11 | My Week & Hobbies | "**On Monday** I have school. I play football **every** Saturday." | "What do you do on Sunday? When do you ___?" | long *i* (kite, night) |
| 12 | Feelings & Health | "I'm happy/tired/sick. I have a headache. You **should** sleep." | "What's the matter? How do you feel?" | long *o* (boat, nose) |
| 13 | Yesterday (past tense 1) | "Yesterday I **played**. I **watched** TV. I **was** happy." | "What did you do yesterday?" | long *u* (cube, moon) |
| 14 | A Story I Read | Irregular past: "**went, saw, ate, had**. The boy went to the forest…" | "Where did he go? What did he see?" | *ee/ea* (green, eat) |
| 15 | Tomorrow & Plans | "Tomorrow I **am going to** visit grandma. I **will** be 7 soon." | "What are you going to do?" | *oo* (book, food) |
| 16 | 🎉 Big Review + Show | He gives a 1-minute "speech" about himself, family, week, and a story. | He interviews a family member in English. | review all sounds |

After week 16: chapter books (Oxford Reading Tree level 2–3), short cartoons in English with discussion, themes from [subjects/main_subject.md](subjects/main_subject.md) at sentence level.

## 🎮 Game Bank — Level 2 (no cards needed — just paper, pen, and the room)

1. **20 Questions** – You think of a secret word (animal, food, object in the room); he asks yes/no questions: "Is it an animal? Can it fly? Is it big?" Then swap — he thinks, you ask.
2. **Detective** – Hide a real object (toy, spoon); he asks "Is it under the table? Is it in the box?" (prepositions).
3. **Mime & Ask** – You mime an action; he must ask "Are you swimming? Are you cooking?" until correct.
4. **Sentence Builder Race** – Tear paper into 15 scraps, write one word on each (I / he / is / am / running / happy…). Timer: build 5 correct sentences in 2 minutes.
5. **Wrong Sentence!** – You say a wrong sentence ("The fish can fly"). He shouts "No!" and corrects it: "The fish can't fly. The fish can swim!" (Big list in the [Question Bank](lessons/quickfire_questions.md), section 9.)
6. **Interview the Toy** – He interviews a stuffed animal (you voice it): minimum 5 questions, then reports: "His name is Bear. He likes honey."
7. **Story Chain** – Take turns adding one sentence each to build a silly story. Past tense weeks: story must be in past tense.
8. **Shop with Money** – Real role-play with real things from the kitchen: "How much is the apple?" "It's three dollars." "Here you are." "Here's your change."
9. **Picture Talk** – Open a picture book or a photo on your phone (street, zoo, family trip). He says as many full sentences as he can in 1 minute. Count them — beat last week's record!
10. **Spelling Bee Battle** – You and he take turns spelling words. Wrong spelling = funny penalty (hop 3 times).
11. **Room Hunt** – "Find something red / something round / something you can write with!" He brings it and says a full sentence: "This is a red cup. It's on the table."
12. **Speed Round** – 2-minute timer, rapid questions from the [Question Bank](lessons/quickfire_questions.md). Count correct full-sentence answers, write the score on the wall calendar, beat it next session.

**👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Whole-family games** (3+ players — Hot Seat, Family Quiz Night, Who Am I?, Stop the Bus, Treasure Hunt, Family Olympics and more): see the [Family Game Bank](lessons/family_games.md). One fixed Family English Night per week works wonders.

## ✏️ Reading & Writing Track (runs alongside, 10 min inside each session)

- **Weeks 1–5:** read & write CVC words (cat, bed, pig, dog, sun) and one short sentence.
- **Weeks 6–10:** read 5-sentence mini-texts; write 2 sentences from memory (dictation).
- **Weeks 11–16:** read a half-page story; write 3–4 sentences about his day ("mini diary").
- Keep one **English notebook**: date in English at the top of every page (Monday, June 10th).

---

## ✅ Weekly Checklist

- [ ] Session 1: New vocab + Grammar A + reading + writing
- [ ] Session 2: Grammar B (questions) + games + free talk
- [ ] Session 3 (optional): Story reading, dictation, role-play, watch a short English cartoon and retell it in 3 sentences
- [ ] Daily 5 min: he asks YOU one question in English at dinner (he must ask, not answer)
- [ ] End of week: 2 lines in the mini diary

---

## 📈 How to know it's working

By the end of each week he can:
1. **Say + spell** the 10–12 new words and use each in his own sentence.
2. **Make 5 original sentences** with Grammar A (not memorized — his own ideas).
3. **Ask** the week's questions, not just answer them.
4. **Read** the week's sentences aloud and **write** at least one from memory.

If a week is too hard, split it into two weeks. If it's still too easy — tell me and we go to Level 3 (short paragraphs, storytelling, opinions: "I think… because…").
